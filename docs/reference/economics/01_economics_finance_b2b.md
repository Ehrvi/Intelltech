## 1. Financial Modeling

### Net Present Value (NPV)
- **Formula:** 
  \[
  NPV = \sum \frac{C_t}{(1 + r)^t} - C_0
  \]
  Where \( C_t \) = cash inflow during the period, \( C_0 \) = initial investment, \( r \) = discount rate, \( t \) = number of time periods.

- **Calculation Steps:**
  1. Estimate future cash inflows from the investment.
  2. Determine the appropriate discount rate.
  3. Calculate the present value of each cash inflow.
  4. Subtract the initial investment from the total present value of inflows.

- **Example for SHMS Investment:**
  Assume an initial investment of $500,000 for SHMS, expected cash inflows of $150,000 annually for 5 years, and a discount rate of 10%.
  \[
  NPV = \frac{150,000}{(1+0.10)^1} + \frac{150,000}{(1+0.10)^2} + \ldots + \frac{150,000}{(1+0.10)^5} - 500,000
  \]
  Calculate each term to find NPV.

### Internal Rate of Return (IRR)
- **Formula:** 
  \[
  0 = \sum \frac{C_t}{(1 + IRR)^t} - C_0
  \]
  (IRR is the discount rate at which NPV equals zero)

- **Interpretation:** The IRR represents the projected rate of growth an investment is expected to generate, useful for comparing profitability.

- **Example:**
  Using the above SHMS example, find the IRR where NPV is zero using financial software or trial and error.

### Payback Period
- **Simple vs Discounted:**
  - Simple: Time taken for initial investment to be repaid from net cash inflows.
  - Discounted: Considers the time value of money in calculating the payback period.

- **Calculation:**
  - Simple: Sum annual cash flows until they equal the initial investment.
  - Discounted: Sum present values of cash flows until they equal the initial investment.

- **Example:**
  For SHMS with cash inflows as above, calculate the time to recover $500,000 using both methods.

### Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)
- **Methodology:** Estimate future cash flows and discount them to present value.
- **Assumptions:** Assumes cash flows are predictable and discount rate is known.
- **Example for 10-year SHMS Contract:**
  Estimate cash flows over 10 years, apply a discount rate, and sum the present values to evaluate the investment's worth.

## 2. Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) Analysis

### Framework
TCO analysis provides a comprehensive view of the total cost associated with purchasing and operating a product over its lifecycle.

### Cost Categories:
- **Initial Investment (CAPEX):** Upfront costs for acquiring SHMS.
- **Operating Costs (OPEX):** Regular expenses needed to operate SHMS.
- **Maintenance:** Costs related to maintaining SHMS functionality.
- **Training:** Costs for training staff to use SHMS.
- **Downtime:** Costs associated with SHMS downtime.
- **Replacement:** Costs for upgrading or replacing SHMS components.

### TCO Calculation Template
1. List all cost categories.
2. Estimate costs in each category over the system's lifecycle.
3. Sum the costs to determine TCO.

### SHMS vs Manual Monitoring TCO Comparison
Compare the TCO of SHMS with traditional manual monitoring solutions to highlight cost savings and efficiency gains.

## 3. Value-Based Pricing

### Concept
Pricing strategy based on the perceived value to the customer rather than the cost of the product.

### Value Drivers for SHMS:
- **Risk Mitigation:** Avoidance of catastrophic failures.
- **Compliance:** Ensures adherence to standards like GISTM and ANCOLD.
- **Efficiency:** Reduces the need for frequent manual inspections.
- **Data Insights:** Facilitates predictive maintenance through analytics.

### Pricing Calculation Methodology
1. Determine the total value derived from SHMS.
2. Assess customer's willingness to pay based on value.
3. Set a price that reflects the value provided.

### Value Communication
Present clear, quantifiable benefits to potential clients to justify pricing strategies.

## 4. Pricing Strategies

### Types of Pricing Strategies
- **Penetration Pricing:** Low initial price to gain market share.
- **Skimming Pricing:** High initial price targeting early adopters.
- **Tiered Pricing:** Different price levels based on features or usage.
- **Freemium:** Basic features free, premium requires payment.

### When to Use Each
- Use penetration for market entry.
- Skimming suits innovative products with little competition.
- Tiered pricing works for diverse customer needs.
- Freemium is effective for software with scalable features.

### SHMS Pricing Recommendations
Consider using tiered pricing to cater to different organizational sizes and needs.

## 7. Contract Structures

### Types of Contracts
- **SaaS Subscription:** Periodic payments for service access.
- **Perpetual License:** One-time payment for indefinite use.
- **Usage-Based:** Charges based on system usage.
- **Hybrid Models:** Combines elements of the above.

### Pros/Cons of Each
- SaaS offers regular revenue but requires continuous service.
- Perpetual provides immediate revenue but less predictable income.
- Usage-based aligns cost with usage but may deter high usage.

### Recommendations for SHMS
Consider SaaS or hybrid models to leverage ongoing service and support.
